Mockups for the three ideas you approved. Each can ship independently. Photos are placeholders from the existing site assets.

Concept 1

Availability status

Actors set one of three states; it renders live on their card, public profile, and in the talent directory. CDs filter by it. Premium actors get "auto-expire" (status flips back to Available on a date) — no stale "on set" cards.

The three states (actor picks in My Profile)

● Available nowGreen. Default. Shows on card + directory; CDs can filter to "available this week".
● On set until Jul 3Amber, with date. Signals in-demand (social proof!) while telling CDs when to come back. Auto-expires back to green.
○ Not bookingQuiet gray. Profile stays visible but card says not currently taking submissions.
Why it converts: "On set until…" makes working actors look booked (status), and CD-side availability filtering is a feature no indie-focused competitor surfaces this simply.
Concept 2

Live card preview before the paywall

Today a free user clicking "Create My Actor Card" lands on pricing without ever seeing their card. Instead: build the card live from their real profile (name, headshot, location), watermark it, and put the upgrade button under the thing they already want.

Flow change

Now: Manager Mode page → "Create My Actor Card" → pricing page (desire dies).
Proposed: Manager Mode page → "Preview My Card" → their actual card, watermarked → "Activate" → checkout. Classic endowment effect: people pay to keep what already exists.
Requires: logged-in profile data only — no new schema. Logged-out users see the generic sample card with "Sign up free to preview yours."
Concept 3

The card as an active submission tool

Today the card is a digital business card. The upgrade: anyone holding the card link can act on it — request a self-tape, invite the actor to a casting, or message — making every shared card a working inbound channel for the actor.

How it works

CD with a CastSlate account: "Invite to My Casting" lists their open roles → one click creates a project invite (the existing project_invites table).
Industry person without an account: the buttons open a 30-second guest form → lands in the actor's inbox tagged "via your card" → nudges the recipient to create a free CD account.
Why it's a USP: the card stops being a static link and becomes the actor's personal submission funnel — "my card got me auditions" is a story actors tell other actors.
📌 Recommended ship order: 2 → 1 → 3. The live preview is pure conversion lift with no schema changes; availability adds one profile column + filter; the submission tool is the biggest build (guest flow + invite wiring) but the strongest differentiator.

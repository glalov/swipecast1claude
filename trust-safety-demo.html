Trust & Safety

Audition smarter. Stay protected.

CastSlate is built to help actors find opportunities without blurring the line between casting, paid services, representation, and unsafe requests. This page explains what should be free, what should be questioned, and what should be reported immediately.

Core Rule

You never pay to audition.

A casting opportunity should not require an application fee, audition fee, callback fee, reading fee, or monthly payment to be eligible for the role. Paid CastSlate services, if any, are separate from casting eligibility.

Platform Standards

What CastSlate separates clearly

Actors should never have to guess whether they are applying for a role, buying a service, joining a class, paying for promotion, or communicating with someone outside the platform.

Casting eligibility

Your ability to be considered for a casting must not depend on paying the casting party. If a role requires money to submit, audition, read, hold a callback, or stay eligible, report it.

Paid services

Memberships, classes, profile tools, coaching, business cards, or other paid products must be presented separately from whether a casting team can consider you for a role.

No employment guarantee

CastSlate can help organize discovery and submissions, but it does not guarantee auditions, callbacks, representation, employment, payment, bookings, or career outcomes.

Compensation Clarity

Every listing should make compensation easier to understand

Castings may vary widely: some pay a day rate, some are student or no-budget projects, some offer copy/credit/meals, and some are deferred. What matters is that actors are not misled about what is known.

Paid
The listing states a rate, range, stipend, contract structure, usage terms, or paid classification. Actors should still confirm the final rate before accepting work.
Unpaid
The project does not offer cash compensation. It may offer copy, credit, meals, footage, experience, festival exposure, or other non-cash value. Actors decide whether that is acceptable.
Deferred
Payment may depend on future financing, sale, distribution, grant funding, or revenue. Deferred pay should not be treated as guaranteed unless a written agreement confirms the terms.
Not yet confirmed
When compensation is unclear, users should ask for written clarification before auditioning, traveling, performing, signing releases, or providing additional materials.
Actor Scam Red Flags

Requests actors should question immediately

Not every unusual request is malicious, but the following patterns are common in scams, coercive casting situations, fake representation offers, or unsafe auditions.

“Pay me to represent you”

Legitimate agents generally earn commission from work they help you book. Be cautious of anyone claiming to be an agent who asks for upfront representation fees, monthly payments, activation fees, or “pay me $200 a month and I will be your agent.” That is not standard agency representation.

  • Real representation should be documented in a clear agreement.
  • Agents typically earn only when you earn, subject to applicable law and contract terms.
  • Paid coaching, photography, classes, or management services must be clearly separated from representation.

Undressing or sexualized audition requests

No one should pressure you to undress, remove clothing, perform sexual material, show private body parts, simulate intimacy, or record intimate material during an audition unless the project has clearly disclosed intimacy/nudity requirements and appropriate safety protocols in advance.

  • Never comply with surprise nudity or private “prove you are comfortable” requests.
  • Ask whether an intimacy coordinator, closed set, written disclosure, and consent process are in place.
  • Minors should never be asked for sexual, nude, or intimate audition material.

Filming without consent

You should know when you are being recorded, what the recording is for, who will see it, and whether it will be stored or shared. Secret filming, pressure to send private videos, or refusal to explain recording use should be reported.

Unsafe locations or private meetings

Be cautious about auditions in hotel rooms, private apartments, isolated locations, cars, late-night meetings, or locations that do not match the production details. When possible, bring a trusted person, tell someone where you are going, and keep communications documented.

Checks, deposits, and money movement

Do not cash a check and send money back. Do not buy equipment from a “required vendor” before meeting a verified team. Do not provide banking details, tax forms, or identity documents until you have verified the production and understand why the information is required.

Pressure to leave the platform

Moving to email, text, or social media is not always wrong, but suspicious users often push actors off-platform quickly to avoid reporting systems. Keep records and report inappropriate contact, harassment, threats, payment demands, or sexual requests.

Reporting

How suspicious castings are handled

CastSlate may review, hide, edit, archive, reject, or remove castings and accounts when they appear unsafe, misleading, fraudulent, exploitative, discriminatory, sexually inappropriate, or inconsistent with platform rules.

Capture details

Save the casting title, messages, usernames, screenshots, phone numbers, emails, payment requests, and any off-platform links.

Report in context

Use the report option on the listing, profile, message, or support channel so the team can locate the exact issue faster.

Stop engaging

If a request feels unsafe or coercive, do not continue the conversation while waiting for review. Do not send money or private material.

Escalate danger

If there is immediate danger, threats, stalking, exploitation, or a minor is involved, contact local emergency services or appropriate authorities.

Parent & Minor Safety

Extra caution for performers under 18

Guardian involvement

Minors should use CastSlate only with parent or guardian consent and supervision. A parent or guardian should review listings, communications, locations, role requirements, and any requested materials.

Hard stop requests

Minors should never be asked for nude, sexual, intimate, secret, private, or coercive audition material. Any such request should be treated as serious and reported immediately.

When in doubt, pause.

No role is worth compromising your safety, identity, money, consent, or personal boundaries. A legitimate production can answer reasonable questions about who they are, what they are filming, where it happens, what it pays, and why they need your information.
